Robustness of Maryland Watermark

Introduction

The Whitehouse discussed regulations in 2023 with leading companies in AI. This led to regulations with the AI Bill of Rights as well as pledges for watermarking AI content. (Reuters Article).
This post will be focusing on generative-text and, in particular, the article looks at the proposal of the Maryland Watermark (arXiv).
As part of my Bachelor's dissertation, I investigated the robustness of the Maryland Watermark through attacking techniques, such as paraphrasing and word-replacement.

This article is an overview of my dissertation and will not go into the technical details. Click here for the dissertation.

Objective

Analyse the difficulty for attacking techniques, such as paraphrasing and word replacement, to remove the Maryland Watermark.

Method

To assess the Maryland watermark, text was generated using the watermark and consequently attacked with three different techniques. The attacking techniques can be summarised as follows:

  1. Paragraph-based Paraphasing (paraphasing entire paragraphs in context)
  2. Sentence-based Paraphrasing (paraphrasing sentences in context)
  3. Word Replacement
The effectiveness of the attacks is determined through watermark detection completed before and after the attack.

To complete paragraph-based paraphrasing, I finetuned a Seq2Seq model, google's t5-large-nl32, with the use of the Par3 dataset. Word Replacement was completed through POS tagging (Model) alongside the WordNet corpus (WordNet).
Sentence-based paraphrasing was completed using a finetuned model, available on HuggingFace.

Research Questions

  1. Does paraphrasing recursively degrade accuracy in detection of the Maryland Watermark?
  2. Is sentence-based paraphrasing more effective than paragraph-based paraphrasing when dealing with the removal of the Maryland Watermark?
  3. Is a low-cost, word-replacement algorithm sufficient to remove the Maryland Watermark?
  4. Are the attacking methods feasible for use within an academic context with respect to the Maryland Watermark?

Results

Summarily, I shall answer the research questions without diving into analysis.

  1. Paraphrasing recursively does not degrade accuracy in detection of the Maryland Watermark, linearly or non-linearly.
  2. I argue that the paraphraser does not matter, despite paraphraser performing slightly better.
  3. The word replacement method was wholly insufficient to remove the Maryland Watermark.
  4. Through a discussion of memory requirements and quality of paraphrasing, I note that sentence-based paraphasing removes the Maryland Watermark to a quality sufficient for use in an academic context.
The actual analysis is available in my dissertation and provides visual and numerical arguments for my results.
